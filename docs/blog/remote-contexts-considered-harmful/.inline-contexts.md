> In [JSON-LD documents](https://www.w3.org/TR/json-ld11/#dfn-json-ld-document), [contexts](https://www.w3.org/TR/json-ld11/#dfn-context) may also be specified inline. This has the advantage that documents can be processed even in the absence of a connection to the Web.
>
> — [JSON-LD 1.1 § The Context](https://www.w3.org/TR/json-ld11/#the-context)

Instead of URL references in the John Lennon document, embed the needed term
definitions directly in the document itself.

<div class="grid" markdown>
<div markdown>
```yaml title="john-lennon-inline-context.yamlld"
--8<-- "docs/blog/remote-contexts-considered-harmful/john-lennon-inline-context.yamlld"
```
</div>
<div markdown>

</div>
</div>

<div class="grid" markdown>
<div markdown>
#### :white_check_mark: Pro
- **Single-file semantics**: The document fully carries the term definitions it depends on
- **No loader policy**: Correct processing does not depend on cache or document-loader configuration
</div>

<div markdown>
#### :x: Contra
- **Verbose**: Large shared contexts such as `schema.org` are repeated in every document
- **No deduplication**: Context updates require re-issuing all documents
</div>
</div>
